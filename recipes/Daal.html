Daal

Description

Daal (also spelled dal, dahl, or dahl) is a term used in Indian cuisine to refer to dried lentils, peas, or beans that have been split and hulled. Daal is a staple food in many parts of India and is a key source of protein for vegetarians. It is often cooked with spices and other ingredients to create a variety of dishes, including soups, stews, and curries.

Daal can be made from a variety of legumes, such as masoor (red lentils), moong (green lentils), urad (black lentils), and chana (chickpeas). It is typically served with rice or roti (flatbread) and is often garnished with fresh herbs and spices. Daal is an important part of many traditional Indian meals and is enjoyed by people all over the world.
